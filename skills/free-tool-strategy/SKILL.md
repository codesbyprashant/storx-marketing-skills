# Free Tool Strategy

## When to Use This Skill
Use this skill when you want to plan, evaluate, or build a free tool 
for StorX — for lead generation, SEO value, brand awareness, or 
community goodwill in Web3 and DevOps circles.

Always read `skills/storx-marketing-context/SKILL.md` first before 
running this skill.

---

## Why Free Tools Work for StorX

Free tools are one of the highest-ROI marketing investments a B2B SaaS 
company can make. They work especially well for StorX because:

- **They attract buyers at the moment of evaluation** — a storage cost 
  calculator attracts someone who is actively thinking about switching
- **They rank on Google** — tools get backlinks naturally, which 
  compounds into organic traffic over time
- **They demonstrate credibility** — building something useful signals 
  that StorX understands the buyer's world deeply
- **They generate leads** — a free tool with an email gate or a 
  "Powered by StorX" footer converts passive visitors into prospects

---

## Step 1 — Free Tool Ideas for StorX

Prioritized by ease of build and marketing impact:

### Tier 1 — Build First (High Impact, Relatively Simple)

**1. Cloud Storage Cost Calculator**
- What it does: User inputs their current storage volume, egress 
  usage, and provider — tool calculates their annual cost vs StorX
- Why it works: Captures buyers at the exact moment they are 
  feeling pain about their cloud bill
- Target keyword: "cloud storage cost calculator"
- Lead mechanic: Show results, then offer "Get a StorX quote" CTA
- Estimated build: Simple spreadsheet logic, can be built as a 
  web page with basic JavaScript

**2. Egress Fee Calculator**
- What it does: User inputs their monthly data transfer volume — 
  tool shows exactly how much they are paying in egress fees 
  with AWS, GCP, and Azure vs StorX ($0)
- Why it works: Egress fees are a known pain point — this makes 
  the pain visceral and quantified
- Target keyword: "aws egress fee calculator"
- Lead mechanic: Email gate before showing full breakdown
- Estimated build: Even simpler than above — pure math

**3. Decentralized Storage Readiness Quiz**
- What it does: 5-question quiz that tells a company whether they 
  are a good candidate for decentralized storage
- Why it works: Qualifies leads, educates prospects, and positions 
  StorX as the expert
- Target keyword: "is decentralized storage right for my business"
- Lead mechanic: Email required to see results
- Estimated build: Simple quiz tool — Typeform or custom page

### Tier 2 — Build After Tier 1

**4. S3 Migration Checklist Generator**
- What it does: User answers questions about their current setup — 
  tool generates a customized migration checklist
- Why it works: Directly addresses the "migration sounds painful" 
  objection — makes it feel manageable
- Target keyword: "s3 migration checklist"

**5. Storage Architecture Analyzer**
- What it does: User describes their current storage setup — tool 
  identifies risks (single points of failure, censorship exposure, 
  cost inefficiencies)
- Why it works: Creates urgency by surfacing risks the buyer may 
  not have considered
- Target keyword: "cloud storage risk assessment"

**6. Node Uptime and Network Stats Page**
- What it does: Live dashboard showing StorX network stats — 
  node count, geographic spread, data stored, uptime percentage
- Why it works: Directly addresses the viability objection — 
  real-time transparency builds trust faster than any brochure
- Target keyword: "storx network status"
- Note: This is also a trust asset for the sales process

## Step 2 — How to Evaluate a Free Tool Idea

Before building any tool, run it through this filter:

| Question | What to Look For |
|---|---|
| Does it attract someone who could buy StorX? | Tool should solve a problem that StorX buyers have |
| Does it have search demand? | Check Google for the keyword — is anyone searching for it? |
| Does it naturally lead to StorX? | The tool's output should make StorX the obvious next step |
| Can we build it in under 2 weeks? | Start simple — a spreadsheet or Typeform counts as a tool |
| Does it demonstrate StorX's expertise? | The tool should make StorX look like the category expert |

## Step 3 — Lead Generation Mechanics

Every free tool should have at least one lead generation mechanic:

**Option A — Email Gate**
Show a preview of results, then require email to see the full output.
Best for: Cost calculators, quiz results, detailed reports.

**Option B — CTA After Results**
Show full results for free, then present a strong CTA.
Example: "Your estimated AWS egress cost is $4,200/year. StorX 
would cost $0 in egress fees. Start your free trial →"
Best for: Simple calculators where results are the hook.

**Option C — Powered By Footer**
Tool is completely free with no gate — just a "Built by StorX" 
footer with a link.
Best for: Community goodwill tools shared in Web3 and DevOps 
communities.

**Option D — Tool Requires StorX Account**
The tool itself is only accessible after signup.
Best for: Tools that are genuinely part of the StorX product 
experience.

## Step 4 — Distribution Plan for Each Tool

Once built, distribute every free tool through:

- [ ] Dedicated landing page on tools.storx.io/[tool-name]
- [ ] Blog post explaining how to use the tool and why it matters
- [ ] Twitter/X thread showing the tool in action with a real example
- [ ] LinkedIn post targeting CTOs and DevOps Leads
- [ ] Reddit posts in r/selfhosted, r/devops, r/CloudStorage, r/web3
- [ ] Hacker News "Show HN" post
- [ ] Product Hunt launch for highest-impact tools
- [ ] Email newsletter feature
- [ ] Add to StorX email signature and onboarding sequence

## Step 5 — Measurement

Track these metrics for every free tool:

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Monthly unique visitors to tool page | Growing month over month |
| Email captures (if gated) | Conversion rate above 30% |
| Tool to trial conversion rate | At least 5% of tool users start a trial |
| Backlinks generated | Tools should attract natural links over time |
| Keyword ranking | Track primary target keyword monthly |
