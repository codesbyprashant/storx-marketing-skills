# Content Strategy

## When to Use This Skill
Use this skill when you want to plan what content to create for StorX, 
decide which topics to cover, build an editorial calendar, or figure out 
which content investments will drive the most signups and brand awareness.

Always read `skills/storx-marketing-context/SKILL.md` first before 
running this skill.

---

## Step 1 — StorX Content North Star

Every piece of content StorX publishes must serve at least one of 
these three purposes:

1. **Attract** — bring the right buyers to StorX through search, 
   social, and communities
2. **Convert** — move a reader from curious to convinced
3. **Enable** — give the sales team something to share that handles 
   objections before the call

If a content idea does not serve one of these three purposes, 
do not create it.

---

## Step 2 — The Three Content Pillars for StorX

All StorX content should sit inside one of these three pillars:

### Pillar 1 — Decentralization and Sovereignty
Content that speaks to the WHY behind decentralized storage.
Attracts CTOs and Web3 Founders who are philosophically aligned.

**Topic examples:**
- Why centralized cloud storage is a single point of failure
- What happens when a cloud provider suspends your account
- The case for decentralized infrastructure in 2025
- How censorship-resistant storage works
- Why Web3 projects should not use centralized storage

### Pillar 2 — Technical Education and Migration
Content that speaks to the HOW of using StorX.
Attracts DevOps Leads who are evaluating technical feasibility.

**Topic examples:**
- How to migrate from AWS S3 to StorX in under a day
- StorX vs AWS S3 — a technical comparison
- How erasure coding protects your data even when nodes go offline
- S3-compatible storage — what it means and why it matters
- How client-side encryption works in StorX

### Pillar 3 — Cost and Business Case
Content that helps buyers justify the switch internally.
Attracts cost-conscious buyers and helps CTOs build the business case.

**Topic examples:**
- AWS egress fees explained — and how to avoid them
- The true cost of cloud storage — a 3-year breakdown
- How StorX pricing compares to AWS, GCP, and Azure
- Building the business case for decentralized storage

---

## Step 3 — Content Formats by Goal

| Goal | Best Format |
|---|---|
| SEO and organic traffic | Long-form blog posts (1500-2500 words) |
| Building credibility | Technical deep-dives and case studies |
| Social sharing and awareness | Short-form posts, threads, carousels |
| Sales enablement | One-pagers, comparison pages, objection docs |
| Community engagement | Reddit posts, Discord threads, forum answers |
| Email nurture | Newsletter issues, drip sequences |

---

## Step 4 — Editorial Calendar Framework

Plan content in 90-day cycles. Each cycle should include:

**Monthly targets:**
- 4 long-form blog posts (1 per pillar minimum, rotate)
- 12-16 social posts (3-4 per week across LinkedIn and Twitter/X)
- 1 newsletter issue
- 1 community post (Reddit, Discord, Hacker News, or relevant forum)

**Quarterly targets:**
- 1 major asset (case study, technical guide, or comparison page)
- 2 competitor alternative pages
- 1 programmatic SEO batch (10+ pages)

---

## Step 5 — Topic Selection Process

When choosing what to write, run each idea through this filter:

**Filter 1 — Search demand**
Is anyone searching for this? Use Google Search Console or Ahrefs 
to check monthly search volume. Minimum 100 searches/month for 
blog content to be worth the investment.

**Filter 2 — Buyer intent**
Does this topic attract someone who could become a StorX customer?
A post about "best decentralized storage for SaaS" = high intent.
A post about "history of cloud computing" = low intent.

**Filter 3 — StorX authority**
Can StorX credibly own this topic? StorX has genuine authority on:
- Decentralized storage architecture
