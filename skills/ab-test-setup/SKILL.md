# A/B Test Setup

## When to Use This Skill
Use this skill when you want to plan, design, or run an A/B test on any 
StorX marketing asset — including landing pages, signup flows, email 
subject lines, ad copy, CTAs, or pricing pages.

Always read `skills/storx-marketing-context/SKILL.md` first before 
running this skill.

---

## Step 1 — Define What You Are Testing

Before writing a single variant, answer these four questions:

1. **What page or asset are we testing?**
   (e.g. StorX homepage, pricing page, signup CTA, email subject line)

2. **What is the single thing we are changing?**
   Only ever change ONE element per test. Examples:
   - Headline copy
   - CTA button text
   - Hero image
   - Pricing presentation
   - Social proof placement

3. **What is our hypothesis?**
   Format: "We believe that changing [X] to [Y] will increase [metric] 
   because [reason based on StorX audience insight]."
   
   Example: "We believe that changing the headline from 'Decentralized 
   Cloud Storage' to 'The Cloud Storage No One Can Shut Down' will 
   increase trial signups because it leads with the censorship-resistance 
   message that resonates most with our Web3 Founder persona."

4. **What is our success metric?**
   Pick ONE primary metric:
   - Signup rate
   - Trial activation rate
   - CTA click-through rate
   - Email open rate
   - Demo request rate

---

## Step 2 — Write Your Two Variants

**Control (A) = current version**
Document exactly what the current version says or looks like. Never 
change the control mid-test.

**Variant (B) = the challenger**
Write the new version based on your hypothesis. When writing copy 
variants for StorX, draw from these high-performing angles:

- **Censorship angle** — "The storage layer no one can shut down"
- **Migration angle** — "Switch in a day. No rewrites. No surprises."
- **Trust angle** — "Even we can't read your files"
- **Cost angle** — "Flat pricing. No egress fees. Ever."
- **Credibility angle** — "Live network. Paying customers. Real uptime."

Always use real customer language where possible:
- Pain: "We need storage that no one can shut down."
- Win: "It just works with our existing S3 setup."

---

## Step 3 — Set Your Test Parameters

| Parameter | Guidance |
|---|---|
| Traffic split | 50/50 between A and B |
| Minimum runtime | 2 weeks minimum — never stop early |
| Sample size | At least 100 conversions per variant before calling a winner |
| Significance threshold | 95% statistical significance before declaring a winner |

**Do not stop the test early even if one variant looks like it is 
winning.** Early results are almost always misleading.

---

## Step 4 — StorX-Specific Test Priority List

Run tests in this order — highest impact first:

1. **Homepage headline** — test censorship/sovereignty angle vs. 
   S3-compatibility angle
2. **CTA button text** — "Start Free Trial" vs. "Try StorX Free" vs. 
   "Migrate in a Day"
3. **Pricing page** — flat pricing emphasis vs. egress fee comparison
4. **Signup flow** — number of fields (fewer = higher conversion)
5. **Social proof placement** — above vs. below the fold
6. **Email subject lines** — pain-led vs. outcome-led
7. **Ad headlines** — decentralization angle vs. cost angle

---

## Step 5 — Document and Share Results

After the test concludes, record:

- What was tested
- Hypothesis
- Winner (A or B)
- Lift percentage (e.g. +12% signups)
- What we learned
- What we test next

Build a simple running log of all tests. Over time this becomes one of 
StorX's most valuable marketing assets — a compounding record of what 
actually works with your specific audience.
