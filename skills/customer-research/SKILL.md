# Customer Research

## When to Use This Skill
Use this skill when you want to conduct, analyze, or synthesize customer 
research for StorX — including customer interviews, survey analysis, 
review mining, Reddit and forum research, and voice of customer (VOC) 
work.

Always read `skills/storx-marketing-context/SKILL.md` first before 
running this skill.

---

## Why Customer Research is Critical for StorX

StorX's two biggest challenges are a brand awareness gap and an 
objection around long-term viability. Both of these are solved faster 
with real customer evidence than with any campaign. Customer research 
gives you:

- The exact words buyers use — which become your best copy
- The real reasons people switched — which become your best ads
- The objections that kill deals — which become your best content
- The proof points that build trust — which become your best assets

---

## Step 1 — Research Methods for StorX

### Method 1 — Customer Interviews (Highest Value)
Talk directly to existing paying customers. These are your most 
valuable research sessions.

**Who to interview:**
- Customers who switched from AWS, GCP, or Azure
- Customers who came from a competitor (Storj, Filecoin, Backblaze)
- Customers who almost churned but stayed
- Customers who referred others to StorX

**How many:** Aim for 5-8 interviews. Patterns emerge quickly.

**Interview format:** 30 minutes, video call, record with permission.

**Core questions to ask:**
1. What were you using before StorX and what made you look for 
   an alternative?
2. What other options did you consider before choosing StorX?
3. What almost stopped you from signing up?
4. What was the moment you decided StorX was the right choice?
5. How would you describe StorX to a colleague in one sentence?
6. What would you tell a friend who was skeptical about switching?
7. What would you miss most if StorX disappeared tomorrow?
8. What is the one thing StorX could do better?

**Listen for:** Exact phrases, emotional language, specific fears, 
and specific wins. These become your copy.

---

### Method 2 — Survey Research (Broad Signal)
Send a short survey to your entire customer base to find patterns 
at scale.

**Keep it short — maximum 5 questions:**
1. What made you choose StorX over other options?
2. What was your biggest concern before signing up?
3. How would you describe StorX to someone who had never heard of it?
4. What is the primary way you use StorX?
5. What is the one thing we could improve?

**Tool to use:** Typeform or Google Forms. Send via email.

---

### Method 3 — Review Mining (No Effort, High Signal)
Mine existing public reviews for customer language. Look for StorX 
mentions or competitor reviews from people who sound like StorX buyers.

**Where to look:**
- G2 — search StorX and competitors
- Product Hunt — search StorX launch and competitor launches
- Reddit — r/selfhosted, r/devops, r/web3, r/CloudStorage
- Twitter/X — search StorX mentions and competitor complaints
- Hacker News — search "StorX", "Storj", "decentralized storage"
- Discord communities — Web3 and DevOps servers

**What to collect:**
- Complaints about competitors (these are your opportunities)
- Praise for StorX (these are your proof points)
- Questions about decentralized storage (these are your content ideas)
- Objections raised publicly (these are your conversion blockers)

---

### Method 4 — Churn Interviews (Critical Signal)
Talk to customers who cancelled. This is uncomfortable but 
extremely valuable.

**Questions to ask:**
1. What made you decide to cancel?
2. What did you switch to and why?
3. Was there anything that could have changed your decision?
4. What would need to be true for you to come back
