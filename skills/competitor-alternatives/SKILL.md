# Competitor Alternatives

## When to Use This Skill
Use this skill when you want to create competitor comparison pages, 
"StorX vs X" pages, or "alternatives to X" pages for SEO and sales 
enablement purposes.

Always read `skills/storx-marketing-context/SKILL.md` first before 
running this skill.

---

## Why Competitor Pages Matter for StorX

Competitor alternative pages are one of the highest-converting page 
types in B2B SaaS. When a buyer searches "Storj alternative" or 
"AWS S3 alternative decentralized" they are not browsing — they are 
actively looking to switch. These pages capture buyers at peak intent.

StorX's awareness problem is a marketing problem, not a product problem. 
Competitor pages are one of the fastest ways to get discovered by buyers 
who are already evaluating the category.

## Step 2 — Structure of Each Competitor Page

Every competitor alternative page must follow this structure:

### Section 1 — Hero
- Headline: Lead with the buyer's pain, not StorX features
- Example: "A Cloud Storage Provider That Can't Be Shut Down, 
  Throttled, or Seized"
- Subheadline: One sentence on S3 compatibility and flat pricing
- CTA: "Start Free Trial" — above the fold

### Section 2 — Quick Comparison Table
Show a simple side-by-side table. Always include these rows:

| Feature | [Competitor] | StorX |
|---|---|---|
| Decentralized architecture | ❌ | ✅ |
| S3-compatible | ✅ / ❌ | ✅ |
| Client-side encryption | ❌ | ✅ |
| Flat pricing / no egress fees | ❌ | ✅ |
| Censorship resistant | ❌ | ✅ |
| Account suspension risk | ✅ | ❌ |

### Section 3 — Who Should Switch and Why
Write 2-3 paragraphs addressing each persona:
- The CTO who wants architectural resilience
- The DevOps Lead who needs zero migration friction
- The Web3 Founder who cannot risk platform censorship

### Section 4 — Objection Neutralizers
Address the top two objections for that specific competitor's audience:
- Reliability: node count, geographic spread, erasure coding explanation
- Viability: active customer count, live network stats

### Section 5 — Migration CTA
- Headline: "Switch from [Competitor] in a Day"
- Body: Explain S3 compatibility = zero rewrites, zero migration pain
- CTA: "Start Free Trial" or "Talk to Us"

### Section 6 — Social Proof
- Include at least one customer quote
- Use verbatim customer language where possible:
  "It just works with our existing S3 setup."

## Step 3 — Positioning by Competitor Type

### vs. Hyperscalers (AWS, GCP, Azure)
- Lead with: Freedom from vendor lock-in and censorship risk
- Tone: Principled, not aggressive — "We are the exit ramp"
- Key message: "You don't have to ask permission to access your own data"
- Avoid: Direct attacks on reliability — they are reliable, 
  acknowledge it, then pivot to control and cost

### vs. Decentralized Competitors (Storj, Filecoin, Arweave)
- Lead with: S3 compatibility and mainstream accessibility
- Tone: Peer respect — "Great mission, StorX just makes it easier 
  to adopt"
- Key message: "Decentralized storage that works with tools you 
  already use"
- Avoid: Architecture wars — buyers don't care about technical 
  minutiae at this stage

### vs. Budget Alternatives (Backblaze, Wasabi)
- Lead with: Decentralization at a comparable price point
- Tone: Direct and cost-focused
- Key message: "Same price range. But no one can shut you down."
- Avoid: Overcomplicating the decentralization story — 
  keep it simple for this audience

## Step 4 — SEO Requirements for Each Page

Every competitor page must include:

- **Title tag:** "StorX vs [Competitor]: Decentralized Cloud Storage 
  Alternative"
- **Meta description:** 150-160 characters, include primary keyword
- **H1:** Match search intent — lead with the buyer outcome
- **URL slug:** `/storx-vs-[competitor]` or 
  `/[competitor]-alternative`
- **Internal links:** Link to pricing page, docs, and signup CTA
- **Word count:** Minimum 800 words per page for SEO viability
- **Schema markup:** Use the Comparison schema type

---

## Step 5 — Sales Enablement Use

These pages are not just for SEO — send them during the sales process:

- When a prospect mentions a competitor → send the relevant page
- In cold email follow-ups → link to the most relevant comparison
- In onboarding emails → reference migration simplicity
